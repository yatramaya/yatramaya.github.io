The Name

What’s in a name?

Yātrāmaya is made up of two words. Yātra and Māya. It is a coerced confluence of two rivers of thought, one where the material meets the mental and physical meets the philosophical.

What’s in a name? Apparently, the universe itself!

A tiny blue coloured speck of dust that seems to forever travel around and away from its star is what we call our home. Planet Earth, our mothership traces a new line around the Sun every second since its existence thus taking us along on this incredible journey that never started and never ends. To limit the content of this website with the English term travel, thus seemed reductive to us. If we peek under the exoticised layer, the eastern word Yātra means travel, pilgrimage, procession, journey and all of that put together. And yet, under the simplest of contexts it also provides the groundedness that we desired in the physical limitations that we encounter on our blue planet.

Māya on the other hand was everything turned inside out. Whether it is the inside-out hypersphere of four dimensional worlds or quite simply the human mind that can imagine its own universes, the word Māya seemed appropriate for a situation where we simply cannot mimic and share what we all feel and experience in our lives. Travel and travelogues are words we use carefully and hesitantly because these aren’t separate from what, when and how we experience them and most of all, who. Our dreams, thoughts and experiences aren’t yours and yet we seem to dream up the same locations, breathe the same air and drink the same water every once in a while. Sharing the center stage sometimes in the play that is enacted inside Vishnu’s dream.

What’s in a name? Apparently, practicality itself!

Yātrāmaya was thus born and the union of words now simply means “made up of journeys”. Here somehow in its simplistic communicable form we share these figments of physical-truth and mental-cloud with you. We call these short, long and sometimes extremely-long journeys as Experiences, if only to call them something.

Engagement in disengagement

Quite often and by many, travel is understood as a form of disengagement from what is the material and economic. This isn’t very different from the assumption that someone on a spiritual quest seems detached from the social realities of life; aloof and distracted. Both of these ideas are constructions of culture and tradition that values private property albeit in two distinct forms. In the former - the western form - the individual who does not seem to be productive economically seems like a traveller and in the latter it is the individual who does not seem to contribute towards his worldly duties. In both cases, private property suffers, the word property used in a sense to mean what one “protects” and is “grounded in reality”.

The physical act of moving from one place to another is at the very core of the substance that we are made of. The stardust that we are made of is on an eternal quest to merge and separate from each other and it is not something we have human control on. But even if we keep that elemental urge aside, the emotional need to connect with the larger being is so strong that travel is the only way of actually merging into what we separate from ourselves so much, the nature that surrounds us. In fact, that is the root of the problem. Our vocabulary. The moment we construct that it is nature that surrounds us or is separate from us, there is a need to disengage from a role and engage in the role of traveling. But for a traveler who “is” nature this is a seamless shift and borderless concepts.

Yātrāmaya is about sharing these moments of realization. To call it a commune with nature would only go to explain the physical experience and further perpetuates the idea of duality. To call it a “moment of clarity” would perhaps sound subjective but would be closer. Whatever we call it, it is too large and sacred an experience but worth sharing. That is why we have tried to keep these moments short, diverse and across our home planet. We sure hope that it takes you on your own Yātra which bring you joy and satisfaction.

Dreamwalkers

To navigate a dream isn’t easy. Gravity aside, there aren’t reliable signposts to take us anywhere. But to think like that is to assume that we have understood the physical world completely. How reliable are memories? Was that movie you experienced in low resolution any different from the re-release you saw in high definition? Is watching the moon with the naked eye with your loved one any different from seeing it with the binoculars? Or is that story that your father told you all your life now just a story that you tell others? This world being a gaseous and electric construction of our minds makes it all the more equal to what we call dreams that we experience in our sleep.

If that is the case, then what is travel? What is a personal experience and what is the big deal about sharing it? Is there an active person at one end and a passive person at the receiving end?

We try to explore these questions more often than not, especially while we aren’t lost in the material comforts that are offered by traveling. But more often than not the travel experiences you will find here are more about the internal journeys that we went through during the physical process. It is about accepting the sanctity of the larger self and it is about respect for a larger being than ourselves. It is a sacred experience and chipping off of yet another layer of ego.

Navigating around here

You will probably find this place intuitive enough to look around. We have used the familiar medium of language, words and images to try and recollect and bring you sections of this long journey. It is possible that we shared some of these with you in the physical self somewhere when we collided in this universe. Or it is possible we nurtured and shared the same dream. It is possible that your dream happened to be the physical space in which we experienced it. What is important for us is for you to enjoy and feel satiated by these experiences. Do write to us if you share the same space. You will find our contact details somewhere around here.
