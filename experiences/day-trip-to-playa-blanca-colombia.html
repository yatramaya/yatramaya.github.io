Day trip to Playa Blanca, Colombia

Timeline : 2019 September
Colombia South America Full Day Beach

The thought of white sandy beaches with blue waters felt almost too good to be true. Tourists mostly head to Cartagena to experience exactly this. The Caribbean islands are famous for some of the most pristine and picturesque beaches in the world. We were surprisingly one of the few who were in Cartagena without a clear intention of soaking in the sun on one of these beaches. Reluctantly, one morning, we walked to one of the hostels which was arranging day tours to Playa Blanca, which, in Spanish, simply means the White Beach. Without having a secured booking, we tried our luck while treating ourselves to a sumptuous breakfast at the hostel. Happy to get the last two seats and lucky that they were right at front, I settled down in anticipation of a Caribbean experience. A bridge connects the Island of Baru (which has the Playa Blanca) to Cartagena and it took us nearly an hour and a half but it was a smooth drive. It might have been more adventurous a few years ago when boats were the main mode of transport to this island. Somehow the idea of reaching a place easier makes it a little less enchanting in my view. It also meant that we could expect huge crowds.

Once our bus dropped us off at the parking lot, all of us headed in the direction of the beach, from where we split into our separate ways. As soon as I entered the beach, I had a mixed feeling of exhilaration and dread. The white sandy beach with its blue crystal water was so perfect, but the beach was filled with tourists with hardly any open space, moreover the vendors started engaging with us offering us tours, food and whatever else they could offer. We persisted and walked all the way to the edge of the crowded area when we reached a secluded spot with nobody around and a little tree cover. We immediately knew this would be where we could unwind. We were happy to be away from the main areas, the beach shack and also the vendors, who hovered around tourists in the densest parts. We have always been pleasantly surprised how it was possible to secure a private spot with zero crowds on any beach, if only we are prepared to walk a little longer than everyone else. While we wanted to enjoy the famous Pina Coladas and the sumptuous meals, it could wait a little longer. For now, all we wanted was to soak in the beauty of the clear blue waters and the white sandy beaches. Maybe swim a little as well without getting hassled by onlookers or hawkers.

As we sat on the shore, tired after a swim, just listening to the waves and looking at the birds, I caught sight of a couple on a private boat enjoying their time in the waters. It would no doubt be one of the activities that would have been on offer. I also saw a local guy performing stunts on a jet ski, probably just enjoying himself or using it as a means to impress his future customers and tempt them to a ride. It was a perfectly sunny day, a nice day to be out at the beach. The colours of the water and the sky overhead were shades of blue. After a swim, we relaxed in the shade of some bushes till our stomachs started rumbling. We were forced to leave the serenity of our secluded spot and walk to the epicentre of the action. This time though, the hawkers were probably smart enough to note that we had not just arrived and were probably more interested in settling down for a meal. We walked into one of the beach shack restaurants and ordered fresh pina coladas and some food. We being the lone customers who had chosen to dine in the shack rather than at the beach, we got special attention by the staff. The pina coladas were huge and they were freshly prepared. After a slow and relaxed lunch got back to the parking lot and boarded the bus back to Cartagena. For some, who chose to stay on the island for the night, there were some additional attractions such as a boat trip to the adjoining mangroves or a longer one to Isla Grande for the unique experience of watching bioluminescent plankton (that glowed in the dark). But for the rest of us, the trip ended with the day at the beach.
