When the entire village came dressed in their finery
When the entire village came dressed in their finery

Timeline : 2017 October
India Asia Half Day Culture People

We decided to visit the Kibber Gompa on a day when we heard there was an important event. Not knowing what it was about, we went along, with our camera ready. We were surprised to find almost the entire village there, young and the old, with more joining in. There was an air of festivity and cheer. Not knowing the language, we smiled at whoever our eyes met with.

Kibber Gompa was the highest point in the village giving a magnificent view of the entire valley below.
It was a bright sunny day and we could see an almost child-like excitement among the people.
The anticipation was palpable. Young and old alike, awaiting the arrival of someone important.
Women were dressed in their finest robes and decked with silver jewellery embedded with turquoise and other precious stones.
Musical instruments made of yak leather and fur were being tuned to get them ready for the ceremony. The musicians, who were to play them started to take the centre-stage while the onlookers assembled in a circular fashion all around.
Men were dressed in their long woollen coats and fur caps. All of them offered their prayers first as they walked in before they took their positions in assembly.
Children were dressed in their everyday clothes and seemed to make the most of their time out with their friends.
We joined them as they all eagerly got ready to welcome the Lama from the nearby Key Monastery, an important Buddhist monastery in the Spiti Valley. The Lama welcoming ceremony is an auspicious one for the locals here as it is not often that important Lamas visit.
We got a chance to mingle with the locals as they generously accepted our presence and treated us as one of their own. There were shy smiles, open smiles and wide grins exchanged frequently among us all.
Not knowing the language, we had to come up with our own interpretation of the proceedings, which was not too difficult. Some of the men and women we noticed were wearing the traditional maroon robes, the colour that the religious priests or Lamas wear.
Everyone had prayer beads in their hands as they went around the Gompa offering prayers.
The Lama of Kibber monastery took up a vantage point gazing into the vast valley below looking for any signs of the approaching vehicles.
People impatiently conversed in hushed voices waiting for him to give them a cue to begin the ceremony.
On the other hand one of the senior-most women who looked a 100 at least seemed to enjoy every minute of this wait period all the while catching up with the many generations of village folk.
Musicians lead by an old woman slowly took their positions and started playing their instruments. The old solemnly prayed, while the young enjoyed the music.
It was almost an hour now. Most of the village folks had assembled. There was only one topic that everyone eagerly discussed - when was the Lama arriving?
The Lama of Kibber monastery disrupted the domestic banter when he abruptly walked towards the entrance. There was a convoy visible at the horizon. The music went into a rhythm and grew louder. All the women lined up along the pathway with silk stoles in their hands to honour the special guest.
People watched with eagerness to get a glimpse of the holy man as the convoy of SUVs drove into the road leading to the monastery, raising a cloud of dust behind them. A Lama, with all the bearings that one would associate with a man of position in religious circles, got off the SUV and along with his cavalcade started walking towards us.
Some important people associated with the monastery escorted the Lama while the villagers sat outside and offered their prayers. We could barely see him through the glass window in one of the upper chambers of the monastery.
The music went into a crescendo as the Lama approached the monastery, and played on for some more time as he assumed his seat inside.
The welcome ceremony was over. A meeting was in progress. The locals left one by one. We bid adieu too.
