No one can stop looking at Jardin
No one can stop looking at Jardin

Timeline : 2019 September
Colombia South America Full Day City Culture People Food Historical

I have many a times sifted through my memories to focus on the colourful postcards in my mind’s eye that for me represent Jardin, and I have wondered, what was it that I did there for a couple of days? As simple as it might sound, nothing really. Absolutely nothing if I were to be accurate. Yes. If there is a really good venue for doing nothing, then it had to be Jardin. We had a friendly host who greeted us with a warm smile and some hot Colombian coffee. She also helped us with our laundry and guided us to the best spots. Our homestay, overlooking a river which we could only hear in a gorge below, was a cozy little place with a beautiful garden. A small sit-out outside our bedroom felt like the best seats in an opera house and the performers as if in competition with Jardin itself, wore the most colourful attire. From Toucanets to Tanagers, all sorts of birds that we usually associated with the tropical jungles were actually visiting this semi-urban setting; one particular one, an Emerald Toucanet was gorging on some Banana left for that very purpose by our generous host. Perhaps the fact that Jardin was a valley and was surrounded by forested hillocks had something to do with the fact that I was witnessing these creatures at such close proximity to human habitation.

Earlier, as our bus snaked through the narrow road leading to Jardin, the lush green scenery started making way for small buildings. The first thing that caught my eye was the candy colours. Shocking pink, Bright sunny yellow, Crayon blue, Deep red, Parrot green; these were the colours of doors, windows, stairways, balconies and sometimes the roofs and railings of otherwise whitewashed houses, each one competing with its neighbour. As our bus passed the central square, the plaza as these areas are usually called - this one named El Libertador Plaza - I noticed several more colonial buildings, perfectly colour co-ordinated facing the plaza. Overlooking it, and towering nearby was the town’s main church, the Basilica of Immaculate Conception, a stone structure, which was undergoing renovation work when we visited. The bus dropped us off at a garage in one of the side roads. The homestay was a short walk away from the plaza. The walk itself was enjoyable, through clean, well maintained streets dotted with many eateries and small shops selling bread, groceries, fresh vegetables and fruits.

There were many things to do in Jardin but I was so busy doing the most important thing there, which is nothing. Hence, all I could get myself to actually do worth remembering was to go for short walks outside the town. I only needed to step out a few meters and would start hearing various bird calls. The fact that Jardin was surrounded by so much nature meant that it was also easy to forget that right behind our backs was a bustling town. Short treks took us through dirt tracks and up into higher elevations where there still existed, some wooded country. And these were the perfect locations to spot more birds, most of them, that I could not recognize. The weather had turned as I arrived in this town and a drizzle that was always threatening to promote itself into a rain, cheated me of the opportunities to take out my binoculars or camera. Almost all my attempts to go on nature walks around Jardin ended this way.

Some evenings were spent a bit more productively by sitting at one of the tables outside a bar facing the plaza and being served chilled beer and snacks. An election rally was in full swing over the weekend, with a party atmosphere all around. We walked around the plaza sometimes. A large tree spread its branches far and wide across the plaza providing the green cover. In the central area children were playing, street vendors were selling a variety of snacks and people sitting on the park benches under umbrellas were enjoying their drinks. Hunger begets hunger. Using our helpful app, I found a homely vegan place in one of the by-lanes. It was operated by a friendly mother-son duo. We found that they not just made awesome food but also were generous, kind souls; we sat there and chatted about a lot of things far and beyond Jardin. This place immediately became our favourite hangout where we would end up for all our meals. As for smaller snacks that was an essential during the short walks in and around Jardin, the grocery stores always sold some freshly packed Arepas.

And so life went on. Or did it? Perhaps it paused. That was Jardin. When I was there I couldn’t stop looking at it. When I am not, I can’t stop thinking about it. Given a chance again, I would just go live in that cute little town forever , watching birds all day and sipping coffee!
