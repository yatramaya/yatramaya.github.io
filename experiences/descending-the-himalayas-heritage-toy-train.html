Descending the Himalayas in a Heritage Toy Train : Shimla to Kalka
Descending the Himalayas in a Heritage Toy Train : Shimla to Kalka

Timeline : 2017 October
India Asia Half Day Train

The Shimla - Kalka Toy Train is hardly a realistic mode of transport today. A vestige of the colonial era, it only serves touristic value. But then again, we were tourists and we ought to behave like that. So we decided that it would be a worthy investment of our time to try out the journey in this colourful train that promises eye soothing views of the mountains and healthy mountain air for the lungs. Moreover, it did not cost an arm and a leg.

While waiting for the train, it was apparent that there were many tourists who had the same item in their travel bucket-list. The train was announced to be running on-time but our excitement stretched our waiting time agony.
When the train did arrive, it was instantaneously apparent to us how spartan and basic it was. Just a metal box with wheels and foam seats. It would be what a kid would draw if one had to draw a train.
What was also apparent was that there wasn't any engine at the HEAD end of the train. But that vacancy, obviously, would soon be filled.
It was a photo-op when the engine made its way to the opposite end of where it was when it arrived.
As time passed, people filled up the train. Children got excited and the bodily-adults were peeking out in anticipation. It was only a matter of time before we were chugging downhill
Somewhere down there were all the stations we had to pass through and also the final destination of Kalka
The first few minutes of the journey was adjacent to the highway carving a route through the woods that stood in testimony
Soon, a station appeared ...
... then, another ...
... and yet another. A few of the passengers de-boarded in some of the stations. Some were intending to find their way back to Shimla, having experienced a short journey while others would continue to Kalka and beyond, but now by road.
Each time the train snaked around the curvature of the mountains, there were cheers from the passengers. And so were there cheers when it passed through the tunnels.
All around us were a carpet of green beauty. The roof of a home here, an entire village there. Some of us were busy spotting what we had spotted once during the previous curve. But it was evident also, that we were slowly leaving the mountain life behind ...
... and the train itself was a bridge that was connecting two very different kind of worlds.
Because, somewhere down there Kalka was announcing itself. First as a dot in the horizon and later as a very palpable feeling that is composed of many more buildings, structures, people and vehicles. The green carpet would fold up and invite dull greys and browns. But that was in a few more hours. Till then, we would enjoy what was left of this unique joy ride.
