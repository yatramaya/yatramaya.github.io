Parque Arvi : Nature overlooking Medellin

Timeline : 2019 September
Colombia South America Half Day Nature Forest

Lucky are the people of Medellin that just an hour away from the busy city is one of the most beautiful parks that has not just breathtaking scenery but also some amazing biodiversity. When we ended up a whole day late in Medellin, at night, after our harrowing flight without our bags, little did I know that the misfortunes we faced the previous day would be compensated manifold by allowing us an extra day in the city that was not earlier planned. Medellin would have been just a transit city for us, on the way to Salento, but thanks to our bags not arriving with us, we got to discover this great treasure called Parque Arvi that is easily accessible from Medellin through a network of metros and cable cars. The cable car ride to the park gave me a preview of the wilderness and greenery that this park offers. The ride is quite long, giving us ample chance to view the outskirts of the city initially, then, moving to lush green landscape and mist covered hills.

The entrance to the park has a food court, stalls to purchase snacks, a booking counter, wash-rooms and friendly staff who can help with our queries. We were keen on going for a half-day trail deeper into the park, however, due to insufficient co-trekkers, we couldn’t gather the minimum group to embark. We decided to go on our own along the main park road, which itself was enjoyable, at our own pace. The trail initially goes along the main road itself, taking a few detours to visit some side-attractions like a fossil that we saw. Wanting to go deeper, we took the main trail. Initially, the weather was pleasant. The cool air and the mist was refreshing. The tall trees provided an excellent habitat for birds. We spotted a Peregrine falcon perched on one of the lower branches. I was already enjoying our little walk, in anticipation of the wildlife that I would encounter further.

Just as we walked past some restaurants, the road turned left and we walked further along. Soon, the bright day started to look overcast. I could feel a drop of rain on my nose. Dismissing it as a passing shower, we walked on. Suddenly, what started as a drizzle soon turned into a huge downpour. Even with raincoats on, I felt it was pointless to continue as rains would not allow us to enjoy the views. We retraced our route walking towards a shelter that I had seen on the way. Standing here, I could experience the raw beauty of this magnificent park. The huge trees swayed in the wind and rain poured, forming a sheet all around us. Water was gushing all around on the small slab of stone we were perched on, with the level rising each passing minute. It was almost noon and I was tired. We decided to walk to the restaurant that was about a hundred meters from where we were standing. Some warm food and shelter from the rain was what I was looking for.

The small restaurant was welcoming. I ordered some hot coffee and tried to dry my clothes as I waited. We ordered the Bandaje Paisa, which is the national dish of Colombia, that is a full plate-meal, with rice, Arepas, fried banana, eggs, beans, salad and meat. We asked for a vegetarian version. It was warm, delicious and was filling. With freshly renewed energy, we decided to walk back to the park entrance, as any delay might mean a longer wait for the cable car. Although we couldn’t walk through the vast park, this experience was precious to me. The trek was something we would have to keep for another time. For now, I cherish the sights and the experience of rain in this pristine natural park.
