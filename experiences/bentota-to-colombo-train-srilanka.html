Bentota to Colombo by train, Sri Lanka
Bentota to Colombo by train, Sri Lanka

Timeline : 2018 January
Sri Lanka Asia Half Day Train

Local train journey in Sri Lanka is a must do to see the culture up close. And, what’s better than the coastal rail route that offers some breathtaking views of the coast as well. Our started at this quaint little station called Bentota.

Bentota is a pretty little coastal town that has some of the cleanest and least crowded beaches.
Not all trains stop here. We chose to travel slow on the passenger train in the afternoon. The station was not very crowded.
The railway station is very close to the town and just a few steps walking distance from where we stayed.
The train was traveling from way down South from Galle and was already full when we boarded it. The tickets were bought just a few minutes before the train arrived.
Traveling in the general compartment, which is known as unreserved, I couldn't get to sit for almost the whole journey, but, got some time standing at the door and enjoying the breeze.
The train passes close to houses, shops, right through some of the small towns. Passenger trains are generally slow enough to get a good view of these towns.
The train was quite old and run down, yet sturdy and functional, using some of the best wood for the windows, doors and seats.
The Sri Lankan Railways, that runs all the way from North till South and along the coast of this small island country is a vestige of the Colonial Era.
The sea is a visible for most part of the journey which offers some of the most beautiful views of the coastline.
The railway line was severely affected by the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami. A train was swept off the track, killing more than 1500, in the worst rail disaster in history.
Children playing cricket is a very common sight across Sri Lanka, it is their national game. Another vestige of the colonial era, apart from tea.
As the train approached Colombo, the railway stations grew bigger with more platforms and more commuters, some of the major ones being Panadura and Slave Island.
Most commuters were locals traveling to and from work. But we did run into some backpackers and travellers who had chosen to brave the crowds and travel in the local trains.
The trains are all converted to Diesel engine. And there are many express and super fast trains as well.
Approaching Colombo, hugging the coastline, the train took some amazing turns to give me a chance to enjoy the coast. The high rises in the horizon indicated that we were close to our final destination.
