Camping next to Savage river, Denali National Park
Camping next to Savage river, Denali National Park

Timeline : 2018 August
United States of America Alaska North America Multi Day River Nature Wildlife

The weather was perfect when we entered Denali National Park. The sun was bright and our bus made a special stop on the way just for us to get a view of the majestic mountain Denali because it is not everyday that she presents herself in such glory. I have heard about the disappointment some people face when they spend two days at the National Park but fail to get a glimpse of the mountain. After completing the mandatory registration and collecting the campsite markers, I got on the park bus that would take me to our campsite, Savage River. It was the farthest point in the park route that allows private vehicles. Then again, we were not driving. We took the transit bus. The campsite was basic yet organized. Without much hunting around, we found the spot marked for our tent and proceeded to pitch it; our home for the next couple of days in Denali National Park.

The best part about Denali National Park was that it was remote. We were in wilderness, amidst wildlife that roamed freely and fearlessly. To camp here was one of the best decisions. Savage river is a smaller camp-ground compared to Riley Creek and does not have anything much other than dry toilets, a covered dining area, and dumpsters. Everything edible had to be safeguarded in a store room with our names on it to keep away the bears. It was cold towards evening and it started to drizzle. Nights were freezing, I could see the frost form on our tent. I woke up in the middle of the night to try my luck with the northern lights. I could barely spend two minutes outside my tent, even with thermals, jackets, gloves, cap and boots. The sky was gloomy, starless. But then, to be out in the open, to hear the sounds of wilderness, to feel one with the elements, even in that biting cold, was an experience I came to cherish for ever.

I was transported to a different world when I decided on short hike into the wilderness, via one of the several trails that began at the the camp-ground Vast green plains with shadows of clouds on the meadows and huge mountains covered by green. Everything in Denali National Park was huge, dwarfing the massive creatures like the black bear and moose. In just about an hour, I had spotted several small birds,a ptarmigan ran into the bushes, a snowshoe hare just crossed our path and eagles flew high above in the blue sky. I could bet that I felt a bear standing just around the bend in the trail. There was so much talk about bears and bear spray, how to avoid a bear attack, that everywhere I went in the park, the elusive bear never left the back of my mind!

After a whole day of excellent wildlife viewing from the bus, all I was looking forward to was crawling into my sleeping bag and slip into deep sleep. At the campsite, we saw a huge owl perched on the roof of the dining area. It did not seem to be disturbed by our movements but that did not deter us from making our best effort to keep a respectable distance. It was a massive great horned owl. The camp-ground was as much his as it was mine. Leaving it in peace to do its owlish things, we took a short walk down to the river. From our campsite, I could hear the river roar. We decided to pay a visit. Wide, with pebble strewn shores and greying glacial water flowing in full force. It was a perfect setting for a camp-ground I stood there for several minutes mesmerised by its beauty.
