Howler Monkeys and the Ultimate Insult

Timeline : 2019 September
Peru South America Multi Day Forest Nature Wildlife

We had walked for almost four hours now. With the dense canopy, it was hard to gauge the exact time of the day. I was starting to become a bit weary trying to keep one foot in front of the other, very gently and as noiselessly as possible. The mosquitoes took advantage of my tiredness and launched a fresh assault. My ears had to stay alert to grasp any sign of monkeys or birds around. Having already spotted our priced viewing of the day - the rare and shy Red Uacari monkeys - I walked with a sense of satisfaction, looking forward to my return to the camp.

Just then, Jose, our guide slowed down and spoke in a hushed voice, asking us to stop and listen. The unmistakable booming sound echoed through the trees, bouncing off and amplifying, giving me goosebumps. It was the howler monkeys. I had only heard them till now, and I was waiting for a chance to spot them and get a clear view of how they were producing that loud sound. I would not be disappointed. We encountered a large group of howler monkeys among the high branches of trees right in front of us. Although we were at a distance, physically, somehow, they detested our presence and us looking directly at them. This did not stop us from taking pictures of them all along. They made their displeasure evident by loud sound, raised shoulders, poising taller. Somehow they wanted to scare us away. But this only made us more interested in their behaviour.

By this time, neither of us could hide the fact that we were, in fact, staring at each other, one irritated and the other amused, yet both not looking away or moving. It felt like a Mexican stand-off. Who would be the first one to make the move? Just then, to my utter shock and disgust, one of them decided to remove any doubts as to what his feelings about us were. He picked up some of his own poop and flung it at us. I couldn’t believe what I was seeing. Never have I been insulted thus by a monkey! He was so deeply anguished that he decided to throw his own shit at us. Thankfully, we were far enough so that it did not land on the intended targets. But this act of displaying one’s feelings was something unique and I had never experienced it earlier. It left me dumbfounded. I did not know how to react. There was nothing much left to say, other than respectfully walk away, which is what we finally did. Not because he threw shit at us, but because we had got enough of his antics captured on our cameras and it had left us satisfied!

In retrospect although, this appeared like the most primeval way to communicate, I felt that it was the closest to a conversation I had had with a monkey. And it could not be clearer than that. He meant to say “You disgust me so much that I will throw my stinking shit at you”. And he did not mince any words, I mean, poop.
