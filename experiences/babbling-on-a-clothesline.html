Babbling on a clothesline

Timeline : 2021 August
India Asia Half Day Wildlife

A wet babbler jumped up on a clothesline under a shelter to escape the downpour. A moment ago she was busy pecking on the wet ground in search of insects and worms. With no sunshine the entire morning, birds were not at their chirpy best, they must be going through their mood swings too! The overcast skies covered everything in dull grey. The babblers still needed to look for their food. She reluctantly ventured out during a tiny break in the downpour. A small respite from the incessant rains. Small, but enough to grab a quick snack. With the sudden showers, she quickly took cover under a temporary shed. She sat there with a smug look, shaking off the water droplets from her feathers. Just then, another babbler flew in and took cover beside her. Though not entirely pleased with this intrusion, she moved closer to get the much needed warmth. Then another joined them. It started looking crowded now; three babblers trying to balance on the now sagging line. Also it started a tiff, a minor displeasure was expressed vocally but soon resulting in one of them turning it’s back and facing the opposite side. So now there were three babblers, all sitting in a row, adjacent to each other, two facing in one direction and between them, sandwiched, one facing the opposite direction. Now the two that faced in one direction suddenly realized they could see each other’s faces and started their own squabble. All the while, none moving away, lest they lose a chance to stay warm. Like old ladies of a family who, knowing very well they need to stay together to survive, yet bickering everyday about mundane household issues.

All this while, staying true to the saying, birds of the same feathers flock together (or something like that), a lone drongo sat by itself a little away from these bickering old ladies, unperturbed and indifferent to the general banter. Neither the drongo nor the babblers tried to communicate with each other. A few seconds later, another drongo flew in and sat next to the other drongo, maintaining a dignified distance between them. They did not communicate with each other. Like aristocrats who might know each other but choosing to stay aloof, displaying a striking difference to the bunch of babblers who continued their banter. The drongos sat there just for a few seconds, till they felt uncomfortable, and one of them flew away. The rain can bring about changes in our own behaviour; some of us who are calm and poised otherwise go into a state of commotion and panic when we experience sudden downpour; some of us who would be docile and demure would start to dance in a childlike manner invigorated by a few drops of rain. Similarly, I have seen some cats who run for cover when they feel a few drops of water on their fur. I was happy to witness such distinct behaviour by a bunch of birds which brought a smile to my face amidst the boredom and gloominess caused by unabated showers.
