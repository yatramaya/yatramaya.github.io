A Night at Southbeach Miami

Timeline : 2019 October
United States of America North America Half Day Beach Culture People

It was our attempt to soak in as much Miami as we could in the short time we spent at South Beach. Night life at South Beach was exactly how I had visualized from my exposure to American films. It was all a grand party, a party that was on every night, without a break. So, we got ready to join this party, first as observers, from the periphery, then making our entry into this fantasy world of neon lights, loud Latino music, dance parties, speeding auto-mobiles and unlimited cocktails.

We took a walk along the main South Beach road, walking on the opposite side of all the action, soaking in the ambience, the theme restaurants recreating Cuban, Caribbean experiences, with fake palm trees, women selling Cubans, vintage cars parked in front of restaurants and guests who sat at the patio enjoying their pina coladas. The place felt surreal. It was unreally-real, a dream of sorts, which I felt I had had several times before, and now was suddenly a part of. After a while, we crossed over to the restaurants side, where we were immediately drawn into the action, friendly staff coaxing and cajoling potential customers, a guy rolling Cubans surrounded by several people just watching him, navigating through the variety of food being enjoyed by guests on the side-walk in front of their favourite restaurants, the aroma of it sometimes making me take quick look at their plates, dance floors that were overflowing with live music blasting out of huge speakers. I was overwhelmed by the choices and wanted to spent a little more time walking before finally deciding on where to spend most of the night. No matter where we would walk in, we were assured of a good time.

After a while, my eyes and ears caught a flamenco solo performance at a Cuban restaurant, and this, more than the menu or the offers on drink compelled me to walk in. We took one of the tables from where we could get a clear view of the performance. It started with a solo guitarist performing a soulful piece, followed by a flamenco dancer who started warming up to the music and displaying her graceful steps. I sat there mesmerized, following her movements. I can’t recollect what I drank or ate. all I can remember now is her riveting gaze, her flowing skirt, the tapping of her footsteps and the soulful guitar music. Without realizing, I had started to move my body to the music, sitting in my chair. When our eyes met, she invited me to the stage and asked me to join her. Without hesitation, to the surprise of my partner, I went up and followed her movements as she taught me a few steps, to the amusement of the rest of the guests. It still feels like a dream. I was not aware of my surroundings, all I noticed was her warm smile, her graceful movements, the music which was flowing through my body and I just knew that I wanted to dance.
