It is raining in Kerala!

Timeline : 2021 August
India Asia Multi Day Nature

The leaves are bright green of various shades, all washed and cleaned. Everything turns green - the plants, trees, walls, the floor, there is fresh growth all around. The trees and plants have all drank to their fill and stored up water for the next season. The soil has soaked up water and is saturated. Small streams of brown water are making their way through previously non-existent routes to lower lands. The water level in the well has swelled up. The paddy fields have flooded and have turned into large ponds and lakes, reflecting the beautiful greenery all around.

The rain is incessant, it does not take a break, it does not reduce in its intensity. The constant “whoosh” sound of pouring water has become a background for all our conversations. Day becomes night and night is the darkest. There is hardly any room for sunshine. There is no announcement of the rain - no lightning or thunder, it suddenly starts pouring without a warning. People stay indoors unless compelled to move out, which they do reluctantly with an umbrella in one hand and the tip of their mundu lifted up with another and carefully avoiding the puddles. This does not stop them from getting wet, however. Everything is wet and slowly all the dry spaces are claimed by a hard-working dampness.

To sit and watch rain is one of the most enjoyable activity that can never get boring as the more you watch the more you discover and learn about the world around. To be patient, to not complain, to shake oneself out of the shackles of productivity, optimization, monetization concepts of the capitalist world and to lose oneself into the mundane tasks of nature, to sharpen one’s observation skills, to be not guilty of lazing can give us unimaginable joy. This is when we start noticing the tiny frogs that are frolicking in the newly formed puddles, the birds that seek shelter under the branches, man-made structures, insects that are crawling up towards drier areas in search of food. For them, rain is a part of life, their lives are tailored around the weather, they seem to act in accordance with the rains rather than fight it. We have a lot to learn from their simple lives!
